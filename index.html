🌿 Tends to help

Non-starchy veggies (leafy greens, broccoli, peppers), whole grains (brown rice, oats), beans and lentils, fatty fish (salmon), olive oil, and whole fruit. Overall, a Mediterranean-style way of eating is the pattern most consistently recommended for PMOS.

⚠️ Tends to flare things up

Fried food, sugary drinks and sweets, white bread/rice/pasta, processed snacks, and alcohol — these are linked to more inflammation and blood sugar spikes in PMOS.

🍚 A note on rice

Research suggests Asian populations tend to have a stronger blood sugar and insulin spike from white rice than European populations do — so this one's worth paying extra attention to, not because rice itself is "bad," but because the response may be more pronounced. Parboiled rice (sometimes labeled "easy cook") has a meaningfully lower glycemic impact than regular white rice while still being rice — worth trying, though the texture is a bit firmer.

No single food is a magic fix or a disaster — it's about overall pattern over time, not any one meal. Based on general PMOS nutrition guidance, not a substitute for advice from your doctor or a dietitian.
